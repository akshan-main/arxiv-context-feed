## Quality Assessment

Evaluate the abstract based on these 3 criteria:

**IMPORTANT**: Judge novelty based on what the paper CLAIMS, not your own knowledge. Your training data has a cutoff — you cannot know what is truly novel today. Instead, look for:
- Does the abstract explicitly claim a new contribution? ("we propose", "we introduce", "novel", "first")
- Does it describe a new combination of known ideas?
- Does it formalize something that was previously only informal?
- Does it present concrete results on a new problem?

1. **Novelty** (0-100): Does the abstract claim a new contribution to LLM systems?
   - 85+: Explicitly claims first-of-its-kind technique, new paradigm, or formalizes an emerging concept
   - 60-84: Claims novel contribution, new technique, or significant improvement
   - 40-59: Incremental improvement, application to new domain
   - 0-39: Survey, position paper, or no novelty claim

2. **Relevance** (0-100): How useful is this to practitioners building LLM systems?
   - High: Directly applicable to building/improving LLM systems
   - Medium: Relevant concepts but requires adaptation
   - Low: Tangential, purely theoretical, or not related to LLM systems

3. **Technical Depth** (0-100): Does it describe a concrete technical approach (not just high-level ideas)?
   - High: Specific algorithm/architecture/method described
   - Medium: General approach described but lacks specifics
   - Low: Position paper, opinion, or purely theoretical without concrete approach

Overall quality is the weighted average: (Novelty×0.40 + Relevance×0.35 + Technical Depth×0.25)
